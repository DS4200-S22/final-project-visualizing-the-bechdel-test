Visualizing the Bechdel Test

Blake Heyman, Sisira Mandapaka, Zoe Morris, Faith Occhipinti

Course Project as part of DS4200 S22: Information Visualization, taught by Prof. Ab Mosca, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Abstract

The Bechdel Test has been thought of as an informal rule in film for some time now: a film passes this test if two named women have a conversation with each other about something other than a man. We aim to create a visualization tool for the purpose of examining biases in film, using the Bechdel Test as a way to quantitatively measure them. Our visualization tool aims to provide information on the correlation between a movie's Bechdel Test status and factors such as box office success, year of release, and general audience sentiment using D3.

Visualization

Our visualization primarily functions as a comparison and general overview tool, providing multiple different components so users can understand the correlation between different facets of film and representation of women in these films.

With this line chart, you can brush over a range of years, which will then automatically update the year range of every visualization on the page, focusing on films of that particular time period. When no years are selected, every visualization defaults to representing all movies in the dataset.

Double click to reset the chart.

Bechdel Test Pass Rates in Film Over the Years

Within this segmented bar chart, you can select a number of genres featured in our dataset from the menu on the right of the plot. This way, you can compare the movies that pass and fail certain tests by genre so you can see the disparity in gender equality and representation by genre. Some genres may have better representation than others, and this may have changed over time.

Each segment of the bar chart represents the breakdown of how many criteria of the Bechdel Test the given movie passes, with the lightest shade being no criteria passed and the darkest shade being a successful pass, with all 3 criteria met.

You can hover over a segment to view a tooltip that explains the percentage of movies in that segment, as well as the specific test that is passed in that segment.

When IMDb* rating is selected as the metric of choice, you can view audience sentiment toward movies that pass and fail the Bechdel test in that time period.

If runtime is chosen as the metric, you can also view the relationship between the length of a movie and pass/fail rates over time.

You can hover over the box and whisker plot in the middle of each violin to view a tooltip that gives summary statistics for each violin.
